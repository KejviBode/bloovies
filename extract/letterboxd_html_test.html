Letterboxd's Top 250 movies, based on the average weighted rating of all Letterboxd users. A few statistics...

The oldest movie on the list is Buster Keaton's Sherlock Jr., released in 1924. The most popular decade in the list is the 1990's with 36 entries. A complete overview:
1920's - 4 entries
1930's - 4 entries
1940's - 14 entries
1950's - 30 entries
1960's - 32 entries
1970's - 25 entries
1980's - 28 entries
1990's - 36 entries
2000's - 35 entries
2010's - 32 entries
2020's - 10 entries

Akira Kurosawa seems to be Letterboxd's most popular director, with 8 entries in the list, of which 4 in the top 40. Next in line with 6 titles is Bergman.

All popular directors:
8 entries - Akira Kurosawa
6 entries - Ingmar Bergman
5 entries - Stanley Kubrick
4 entries - Hayao Miyazaki, Martin Scorsese, Wong Kar-wai, Satyajit Ray, Andrei Tarkovsky, Masaki Kobayashi, Billy Wilder
3 entries - Christopher Nolan, Quentin Tarantino, Alfred Hitchcock, Peter Jackson, Abbas Kiarostami, Francis Ford Coppola, Charlie Chaplin, Sergio Leone, Hirokazu Kore-eda, Emeric Pressburger & Michael Powell, Federico Fellini, Hideaki Anno, Sidney Lumet

Fun fact:
Highest ranked movies in IMDb's top 250 that are absent in the Letterboxd list: Forrest Gump (11), The Matrix (17), The Green Mile (28), Léon: The Professional (36) and Gladiator (37).
